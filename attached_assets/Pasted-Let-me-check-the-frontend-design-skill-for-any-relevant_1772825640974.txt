The core problem was that the old shaders used smoothstep with pow(0.8) for the core, which actually softened it (power < 1 = brighter, not sharper). Combined with the core and corona having nearly identical radii (0.08 vs 0.15), everything blended into a uniform glow with no depth.
The rewrite replaces all cores with layered Gaussians — a blazing pinpoint (exp(-d²*3000)), hot core, inner corona, outer corona, and atmosphere — each with sharply different falloff rates. Intensity at center exceeds 1.0, which additive blending naturally clamps into that overexposed HDR look.
